Market Map

Brokers, exchanges, crypto, and pairs are not the same thing.

The setup experience differentiates each category so users of different ages and backgrounds can recognize what they are connecting to and why it matters.

Brokers

Brokers act as account providers or intermediaries. They may offer stocks, forex, options, funds, or other instruments, and they often control order routing, margin, and account rules.

Exchanges

Exchanges are marketplaces where orders meet. Pricing, liquidity, fees, hours, and regional access can vary from venue to venue.

Crypto Venues

Crypto platforms operate around wallets, tokens, stablecoins, on-chain transfers, centralized exchanges, and decentralized liquidity pools.

Pairs

Pairs compare two assets or currencies, such as BTC/USD, ETH/USDT, EUR/USD, or asset-to-asset routes. Pair selection changes fees, spreads, and liquidity.

Equity Markets

Equity access focuses on public-company shares, ETFs, market hours, broker account types, and the difference between cash and margin accounts.

Risk and readiness

The system explains that price differences can close quickly and that fees, delays, rules, and account limitations can remove an apparent opportunity.
